Keeping up with the team

 

02/13/2017

As our team is approaching our Go/No-Go Presentation Thursday, we are beginning to gather more insights regarding our scope area. We have created a Qualtrics survey, which has open-ended questions and close-ended rating scales so we can gather quantitative and qualitative data. Our survey has been sent out to Social Science research Principal Investigators who've interacted with the Purdue IRB before. Our advisor, Dr. Vorvoreanu has helped us in gathering subjects. Throughout this week and next, we plan to gather more subjects for in-person interviews, specifically Principal Investigators and intake analysts at the IRB. Stay tuned for more information coming soon!

 
 

02/5/2017

We have defined our scope and refined our problem statement/research questions. Our new research question is: "How can we eliminate errors and reduce amount of rejected proposals from PI's throughout initial submission to intake?".

Our team led a brainstorming sessions, in which we identified main pain points and focus areas to help define our scope and starting points.


Throughout the week, we will be gathering subject PIs and begin conducting interviews. From this, we aim to discover what specific constraints PIs are encountering while completing IRB required forms for submission through CoeusLite.

 
 

01/25/2017

The team had its first meeting with our project sponsor, Ms. Nancy Hathaway of the Purdue IRB department. We were able to include a Purdue graduate student in our meeting, who is looking to quantify the time/money lost during the process of research approval. Nancy provided our team with a detailed overview of how the Purdue IRB department operates, its workflow and different staff positions. Currently, we are performing some secondary research on the constraints and issues that typically arise in IRB workflows. With that, we are looking into defining a scope soon, based upon our primary research gathered from PI's, IRB Staff/Analysts. Once our scope is defined, we will dive deeper into a focused solution to the discovered problems. This will also help us refine our problem and research statements.

 
 

01/23/2017

Things are just starting to get going! We have developed this website as a means to relay information to those who may be interested. We are still in the early steps of our project, but have great hopes for what is to come! We have chosen the name Team Affinity, due to all four of our team members being focused in UX design, and that being a staple analysis method!
